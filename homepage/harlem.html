Harlem

What is Harlem?

Of the dozens of diverse neighborhoods in New York City, one of the most famous and most historical is Harlem. Located in Upper Manhattan, Harlem is commonly known as the epicenter of Black culture as well as the birthplace of jazz and hip hop. Harlem is also famously known for its historical figures such as James Baldwin and Ralph Ellison, as well as events such as the Harlem Renaissance and the Harlem Riots

Harlem Renaissance

The Harlem Renaissance was a period of U.S. history marked by a burst of creativity within the African American community in the areas of art, music and literature. For the first time, Black artists and intellectuals were able to flourish in a world previously dominated by white voices. The Harlem Renaissance instilled in African Americans across the country a new spirit of self-determination and pride, a new social consciousness, and a new commitment to political activism, all of which would provide a foundation for the Civil Rights Movement of the 1950s and 1960s.
